# Computational Methods for Modelling Dynamic Social Behavior
**Summer Semester 2026 · University of Konstanz**

This seminar introduces computational and analytical methods for studying dynamic social behavior in political and social systems. 
The course focuses on modeling learning, adaptation, and interaction over time, and on analyzing high-dimensional behavioral data. 
Social dilemmas and collective behavior serve as motivating applications, while the emphasis lies on methods for simulation, pattern detection, and interpretation from a social science perspective.

## Topics

### Block 1 · Reinforcement Learning for Social Dilemmas
**Focus:** learning and adaptation in repeated interaction  
- Reinforcement learning as a behavioral model  
- Cooperation, defection, and incentive dynamics  
- Emergence of collective outcomes over time  
- Simulation-based evaluation of institutional and interaction rules  

### Block 2 · Multidimensional Behavioral Time Series
**Focus:** empirical analysis of dynamic behavior  
- Behavioral sequence and panel data structures  
- Trajectories, clustering, and regime shifts  
- Change-point detection and temporal patterns  
- Robustness, noise, and measurement challenges  

---

## Learning Goals

- Understand computational models of learning and adaptation in social interaction.
- Apply methods for analyzing dynamic and multidimensional behavioral data.
- Interpret simulation and time-series results using social science theory.
- Critically assess methodological assumptions and limitations of dynamic models.

---

## Course Format and Evaluation

- Seminar-style sessions  
- Hands-on exercises with simulated and empirical data  
- Student presentations  
- Written term paper
